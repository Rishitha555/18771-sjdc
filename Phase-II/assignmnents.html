Minicoy Island- One of the most prominent places to visit in Lakshadweep, this island is the central hub that connects you to its sister islands. It lies on the southwest side of the group of islands and is adorned with many attractions. It is known for its white sandy beaches and lighthouse. The 300 feet lighthouse, built by the British in 1885, is a prominent landmark. If you visit this island during special festivals, you will be able to witness their traditional dance“Lava” and colourful race boats called “Jahadhoni”. Kadmat Island- Low population and rich marine life make this island more attractive than other places to visit in Lakshadweep. It is a perfect getaway for people who are in search of serenity. The blue lagoon, sun-kissed beaches and colourful coral reefs add to the beauty of this island. Kadmat village is the only inhabited hamlet on this island and is a popular nesting spot for marine turtles. Local seafood is something to savour and relish here. This Lakshadweep beach is also known as Cardamom Island. It is also one of the best places to visit in Lakshadweep for water sports and underwater activities. Marine Museum- Located in Kavaratti, Marine Museum is one of the most unique places to visit in Lakshadweep. It is home to a variety of marine-related artefacts. With an aim to promote marine products and their preservation, this place provides a lot of information about marine life. It also houses an aquarium with several rare species of fishes and other sea creatures. If you are curious to know about marine creatures, then this is one of the best places to visit in Lakshadweep. The tickets are nominally priced at INR 15 only.

When to visit Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep enjoys pleasant weather all round the year. Winter (Oct to March) is the best time to visit Lakshadweep as the weather is perfect for sightseeing. Tourists can plan a visit to Lakshadweep during the summer months (March to May) as well. During these months it is easy to explore all the places to visit in Lakshadweep.


Bangaram Atoll- Bangaram Island is famous for its breathtaking sunrise and sunset. Pristine sand beaches, calm lagoons and coral reefs are the main attractions here. This Lakshadweep beach glows at night due to the presence of algae which emits light at night. This phenomenon is called bioluminescence. Walking on the glowing Bangaram beach at night is a surreal experience.
Among all the places to visit in Lakshadweep, Bangaram is the only island Lakshadweep where the sale and consumption of alcohol are permitted.

Andretti Island Andretti is another jewel among places to visit in Lakshadweep. It is the largest among all the islands in Lakshadweep and also the closest island from the Indian mainland. Things to see at Andretti Island include Buddhist archaeological remains and the tomb of Saint Hazrat Ubaidullah.
